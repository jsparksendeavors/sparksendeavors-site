Philosophy

The operating system: four Stoic virtues.

Every company in this portfolio, every page of the books, every talk from a stage runs on the same code the Stoics wrote two thousand years ago. Resilience in chaos; integrity in success.

Sophia

Wisdom

See things as they are — not as fear reports them, not as ego prefers them. Decide on evidence, hold opinions loosely, and let the scoreboard tell the truth.

Andreia

Courage

Do the hard right thing over the easy wrong one. Every worthwhile thing here started on a roof at eighteen; nothing worth building has ever been comfortable.

Dikaiosyne

Justice

Deal straight — with customers, crews, and partners. Fair pricing and transparency aren't marketing; they're the reason any of this deserves to exist.

Sophrosyne

Temperance

Success is not the same as peace. Discipline in prosperity, moderation at the peak, and the self-command to want what you already have.

About

Josh Sparks

Josh Sparks started at eighteen with a nail gun and a friend's offer of work on a Milwaukee roof in 1997. The company he went on to found became Infinity Home Services — 25 brands, more than half a billion dollars in revenue, and a standing mission to save communities from unscrupulous contractors.

Along the way he sold a stake to private equity, scaled through two growth transactions, and recently handed the CEO seat to a successor to serve as founder and chairman — trading day-to-day operations for the longer game: investing in the trades, writing, and speaking.

He lives in Franklin, Tennessee with his wife MaryJean and their five children. His life's mission is to live and advocate for Stoic wisdom and entrepreneurial drive — resilience in chaos, integrity in success.
